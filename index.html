Biography

I am a full-time Lecturer in the Department of Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, and Statistics at Case Western Reserve University (CWRU), beginning in Fall 2025. I teach STAT 312/312R (Basic Statistics for Engineering and Science) and MATH 201 (Introduction to Linear Algebra for Applications). My teaching and research interests lie at the intersection of statistics, applied mathematics, and data science, with an emphasis on financial modeling using Lévy processes and applications to the equity markets.

I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and have pursued advanced doctoral study in Statistics (followed). Over the past 13 years, I have taught at both the undergraduate and graduate levels, integrating real-world datasets—particularly from finance and engineering—into coursework to develop modeling fluency, computational literacy, and statistical reasoning. I also completed a Data Science Fellowship (The Data Incubator), where I focused on practical machine learning workflows, data engineering, and end-to-end analytics.

Areas of Interest

  • Time series and predictive modeling for markets and operations

Teaching Philosophy

My approach is problem-driven and computation-forward. Students learn theory in tandem with practice: deriving results, implementing them in code, and validating conclusions with data. I emphasize clear communication of statistical evidence and ethical use of data. In STAT 312/312R, I align R assignments with Montgomery, Runger, and Hubele so that learners build both conceptual and computational mastery.

Selected Projects & Topics

  • Time-series forecasting and risk assessment for equity portfolios
  • Applied ML pipelines (feature engineering, model selection, validation, deployment)
  • Data products and dashboards for decision support in engineering contexts
