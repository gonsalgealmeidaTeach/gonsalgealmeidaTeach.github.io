Biography

I am a full-time Lecturer at Case Western Reserve University, where I teach STAT 312/312R (Basic Statistics for Engineering and Science) and MATH 201 (Introduction to Linear Algebra for Applications). My interests include financial modeling with Lévy processes, algorithmic trading, data science, and applied statistics.

I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and have pursued advanced doctoral studies in Statistics. With over 13 years of university teaching experience, I integrate real-world datasets—especially in finance and engineering—into coursework to develop students’ modeling and analytical skills.
