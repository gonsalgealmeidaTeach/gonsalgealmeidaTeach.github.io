Course Overview

Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to probability models and statistical methods for analyzing data. The primary objective is to equip students with the ability to formulate statistical models, select appropriate methods for inference, and test the model's validity. It offers a balanced approach to probability, fundamental concepts of statistics, estimation, hypothesis testing, analysis of variance, design of experiments, and regression modeling.

STAT 312

Focuses on theoretical foundations and manual calculations.

STAT 312R

Emphasizes the use of R programming for all statistical analyses.

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, you will have a firm basis in probability and statistical inference, be able to apply these methods to practical problems, and (for 312R) perform these analyses using R, including data manipulation and visualization with `ggplot2`.
