OpenIntro IMS – Statistical Inference Readings

This curated reading sequence supports Week 3 learning objectives (LO1, LO3, LO4) by introducing the foundations of statistical inference for comparing population means. These materials provide conceptual background for hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and analysis of variance, which are essential tools for statistical decision-making in multivariate research.

Curated Reading Sequence

  • Reading 1 – One-Sample Inference
    Focus: Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing for a single population mean.

  • Reading 2 – Two-Sample Inference for Means
    Focus: Comparing two independent population means using statistical inference.

  • Reading 3 – Paired Sample Inference
    Focus: Statistical analysis for paired or matched observations.

  • Reading 4 – Inference for Many Means (ANOVA)
    Focus: Comparing multiple population means using Analysis of Variance (ANOVA).

How These Readings Support Multivariate Analysis

Understanding inference for means provides the statistical foundation for many multivariate techniques such as MANOVA, discriminant analysis, and multivariate regression. These readings emphasize the conceptual framework behind comparing groups and evaluating statistical evidence, which later extends to higher-dimensional statistical modeling.

Students should review the readings in order and reflect on how statistical inference supports data-driven decision making in applied research contexts.
